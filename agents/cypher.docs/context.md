## Key Findings

- **Iocane Resistance = Hearts mechanic**: The 2-heart system is the iocane resistance narrative. First poison = survive; second = death. Max 2 rounds.
- **Clue system is oblique adjective matching**: Vizzini NEVER directly references a goblet. He embeds attribute adjectives in insults/compliments. Players match those adjectives to goblet descriptions in the Goblet Phase.
- **Deck system is the anti-contradiction engine**: Attribute variants are exclusive per goblet per round. This prevents contradictory clues and creates near-infinite variety.
- **Buttercup's hints are mechanically useful**: Her hints naturally contain goblet attribute adjectives — a real gameplay advantage, not just flavor.
- **Boy character**: Named "Boy" (Fred Savage). NOT "Sick Boy." Uses `*cough*`/`*sneeze*` emotes. Draws from real movie dialogue.
- **Riddle deck**: HUGE, no repeats, drawn without replacement. Max 6 riddles per game. Riddles serve narrative, not difficulty.
- **Difficulty scaling**: Backlog item only. Riddles are prompts for Vizzini's clue dialogue, not hard puzzles.

## Important Notes

- The clue mechanism is the **core design secret**: players don't realize adjectives in Vizzini's rants are actual clues until they see the goblet descriptions. This "aha moment" is the intended UX reward.
- Safe goblet is randomly assigned each round. No predetermined patterns.
